From a quarantine habit to a lifelong language.

How It Started

Digital art is a hobby I picked up during quarantine and never really stopped. What began as something to fill the quiet hours of 2020 turned into one of the most important creative outlets in my life. There is something freeing about a digital canvas — you can undo, experiment, layer colors in ways that would be impossible on paper, and build entire worlds from a blank screen. My inspiration and passion for it grew from anime, webtoons, and video games. I was drawn into the realm of colorful make-believe, vivid characters with rich backstories, fantastical environments, and visual storytelling that could make you feel things no amount of text could quite capture on its own. I loved fiction, and most of all I loved storytelling. The idea that a single illustration could carry an entire emotional moment always fascinated me. I still keep up with digital art regularly. It sits alongside my CS and Design studies as a reminder of why I care about visuals in the first place — not just as a tool for usability, but as a language. And my biggest dream, my side quest, is to one day write and illustrate my own webtoon. A story told in color, panel by panel, from imagination to screen.

What Inspires Me

The worlds that shaped my art

Anime

The expressive character design, dramatic lighting, and emotional visual language of anime were the first things that made me want to draw in this style. Shows like Hunter x Hunter, Fruits Basket, and Orange showed me how much storytelling lives in a single frame.

Webtoons

Webtoons changed how I think about sequential art. The vertical scroll format, the indie creators building entire universes from scratch, and the direct connection between artist and reader — it all made the dream of creating my own feel genuinely possible.

Video Games

Games like Genshin Impact and Hollow Knight introduced me to environmental storytelling — the way art direction, color palettes, and world design can communicate lore and emotion without a single word of dialogue.

The Dream

Writing & illustrating my own webtoon

More than anything, I want to create a webtoon of my own one day — a story I have both written and illustrated, built entirely from my imagination. It is the ultimate convergence of everything I love: character design, narrative structure, visual composition, and world-building. It will not happen overnight, but every piece I create is a step closer to that side quest becoming real.
