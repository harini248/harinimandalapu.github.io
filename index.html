About Me

My name is Harini Mandalapu. I am a third-year student at Northeastern University studying Computer Science and Design, and I grew up in Warren, New Jersey. From a very early age, art was not something I did occasionally — it was how I made sense of the world. I sketched constantly, filled notebooks with portraits and patterns, painted whenever I had access to a canvas, and found ways to create in whatever medium I could get my hands on. Creativity was not a pastime for me; it was a reflex. When it came time to think seriously about what I wanted to study, I knew I needed a path that did not ask me to choose between the logical and the expressive. Computer Science and Design turned out to be exactly that — a space where both instincts are not just welcome but necessary. I have come to believe that the best technology is not simply functional. It is considered. It is crafted with the person on the other side of the screen in mind, shaped by empathy as much as by engineering. Every course I have taken, every project I have worked on, has reinforced that conviction. The overlap between how something works and how it feels to use it is where I am most engaged, most curious, and most myself. I am always drawn to work that sits at that intersection — where a clean line of code and a deliberate design choice come together to make something that is not just usable, but genuinely good.
